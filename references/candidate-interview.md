# Candidate Interview Reference

## Intake principle

Treat the candidate's free-form description, preferences, worries, values, corrections, portfolio links, and voice transcript as primary material. A resume is optional and never the sole source of truth.

## Living profile

Maintain these sections across turns:

- confirmed experience and scope;
- technical depth and production responsibility;
- public, private, and missing evidence;
- preferences, dislikes, values, and desired environment;
- location, compensation, education, timing, and other constraints;
- target directions and reasons;
- uncertainties and contradictions;
- planned learning or projects that must not be presented as completed.

Attach a source turn or file to material facts. When the user corrects a fact, retain the correction history and use the latest confirmed version.

## Follow-up selection

Ask only questions with high decision value. Prioritize:

1. facts that trigger a hard filter;
2. responsibility boundaries and independent contribution;
3. production scale, reliability, evaluation, and measurable outcomes;
4. confidentiality boundaries and safe evidence;
5. preferences that change company ranking;
6. contradictions between files and later statements.

Ask no more than three questions in one round. Explain briefly what decision each answer affects. Do not block the entire analysis when reasonable partial conclusions are possible.

## Confidential work

Ask for architecture, constraints, trade-offs, validation method, failure handling, and the candidate's decisions at an abstract level. Do not request source code, proprietary datasets, client identities, unreleased algorithms, or sensitive production metrics.

Turn private depth into safe evidence such as:

- problem and operational constraint;
- responsibility boundary;
- alternatives considered;
- validation and monitoring method;
- generalized outcome;
- what can be demonstrated with a synthetic or open-source reproduction.
